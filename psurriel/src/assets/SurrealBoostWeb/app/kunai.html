KUNAI: Similar marketing, different product




KUNAI is a 2D single-player metroidvania whose differential mechanic is the use of two hooks as displacement skill. It is a good product and it has a very differential art style, thanks to its use of desaturated colors. A really similar concept to our product.

However, in practice KUNAI differs greatly from SYNAPSE. It opts for a constrained hook implementation. Each hook has a predefined direction. Its hook has two possible behaviors: swinging or propel you to the surface. But these are never combined or switched each other. It depends on the contact surface inclination.

The level design and AI pretends to encourage a typical platformer movement; Walking and jumping. The hook is a skill to combine with the movement, but not the movement core. It is like combining the classic movement with a dash but with a hook.

KUNAI has really interesting ideas for agility challenges. These can be interesting references for us.

The minimalism in its maps and assets reuse makes much more difficult than usually to have a clear idea of where you are. Causing a great need to check the map constantly.

The product has a very different gamefeel. However, in terms of marketing, it transmits a similar message. We should not worry about product similarity, but we should be concerned about differentiation in our campaigns.
